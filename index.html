What's out there?

Survey respondents noted that a part of the problem is knowing what exists. Long standing projects like snort, bro, suricata, nmap, metasploit benefit from tribal knowlege as well as coverage in certifcations, etc. New projects have things like demo lab at Defcon, arsenal at black hat, but get almost no coverage from press, etc unless they are attached to a tech giant like facebook, google, etc.

5 things you can do with open source!

(that maybe you didn't know..and now you do!)

OK, so a look at challenges and potential solution areas to do more work in. Lets pivot a bit and look at some open source projects you might not be aware of that can potentially help you in your infose program. No particular order.. not a beauty contest ;-]

Map yer cloud

https://github.com/duo-labs/cloudmapper

Easy way to get a handle on your AWS environment: - did that cloud formation template work? - did the intern spin up bitcoin miners? - did you remember to turn down that stack of expensive ec2 instances?

JA3

SSL fingerprinting

SSLVersion, Cipher, SSLExtension, EllipticCurve, EllipticCurvePointFormat

TLS fingerprinting of the client hello,

JA3

Examples of the fingerprints. useful for: - you have an app (mobile) and an api, whitelist what talks to it - old versions of your app/browser in play? - monitoring your own infra for rogue traffic

JA3-whew!

Pwnage?

Easily check data exposure: https://github.com/brycx/checkpwn

Using the k-anonymity mathmatical property to take the first x chars of the hash, get a list of 3-400 potential matches and search within that on the client side such that your password is never sent.

Binary Alert

Easy s3/yara https://github.com/airbnb/binaryalert

(relatively easy)

aws rube-goldberg machine made easy to send files to s3 buckets to be analyzed against your own set of yara signatures.

Falco

sysdig's falco

falco is syscall monitoring with really nice rulesets to gain visibility and alerting on actions within containers without having to modify the containers.

bonus!

I said 5, but everyone likes a bonus jonas..

gltail

https://github.com/Fudge/gltail

old school file tailing with a modern open gl visualization. with gltail, if you can ssh into it.. you can visualize it.
